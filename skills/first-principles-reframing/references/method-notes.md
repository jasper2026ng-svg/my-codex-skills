## Why This Book Was Chosen

Use this book as the first source because it directly targets first-principles thinking and has an explicit practical frame. Other shelf candidates such as "打开心智", "底层逻辑", and "元思考力" are useful context, but they are broader cognitive-thinking books rather than a narrow first-principles tool book.

## MVP Scope

This first skill is not a full book conversion. It only extracts one narrow working skill:

> Challenge a current solution or inherited practice by separating facts from assumptions, identifying true constraints, and rebuilding a testable solution space.

Do not expand this skill into generic creativity, innovation, philosophy, or "think different" advice unless later tests show that helps.

## Useful Source Anchors

Use these chapter anchors for future refinement. Do not quote the book at length.

- `1 什么是第一性原理`: distinguish basic facts from copied analogies.
- `2 从火箭到电池`: use well-known innovation cases as reminders to question cost structure and inherited limits.
- `3 第一性原理的思维框架`: strip assumptions, locate fundamentals, rebuild.
- `4 第一性原理在日常生活中的应用`: apply to practical life choices, not only technology.
- `5 第一性原理与AI时代`: keep humans responsible for problem definition and judgment.
- `6 第一性原理在工作中的实战指南`: use in work redesign and decision challenges.
- `7 练习第一性原理的五个习惯`: convert thinking into repeated practice.
- `8 第一性原理的边界与谦逊`: do not challenge everything; respect constraints, expertise, and uncertainty.

## Extraction Rules

- Paraphrase methods; do not copy source prose into deliverables.
- First classify claims before challenging them.
- Do not confuse "basic fact" with "strong opinion."
- Do not force radical redesign when a small test is enough.
- Treat culture, incentives, local market constraints, and human behavior as real facts when they can be observed.

# Human Learning Card: HRBP Business Diagnosis

Linked agent skill: `hrbp-business-diagnosis`

## What This Helps You Learn

Translate HR symptoms into business and organization diagnosis before choosing HR actions.

## Core Human Judgment

The key skill is not naming an HR module. It is seeing what business outcome is blocked, whether the cause sits in people, roles, managers, incentives, process, or the wider system, and where HRBP can create leverage without replacing business ownership.

## 3 Principles To Internalize

1. Start from the business outcome, not the HR symptom.
2. Separate people, role, and system causes before blaming attitude or capability.
3. Use HR data to test a business hypothesis, not to decorate an HR opinion.

## When To Use

- Turnover, morale, performance, staffing, capability, or execution issues are affecting business results.
- A business manager asks HRBP for help but the real problem is still unclear.
- You need to decide whether the issue is people, role design, manager behavior, incentives, process, or structure.
- You need to turn an HR view into a recommendation business leaders can act on.

## When Not To Use

- The issue is mainly a legal, compliance, or labor dispute.
- The user only needs an HR template or policy wording.
- There is no business context, target, team reality, or evidence to diagnose from.
- The problem is purely individual coaching with no organization or business implication.

## Before Asking The Agent

Write your 30-second initial judgment:

```text
The HR symptom is...
The business outcome affected is...
My current guess is that the root issue is people / role / system because...
The first evidence I would check is...
The business manager must own...
HRBP should own...
```

## After The Agent Responds

Compare:

- Did the agent translate the symptom into a sharper business question?
- Did it separate people, role, and system causes better than your first judgment?
- What evidence would change the diagnosis?
- Which intervention is smallest but most likely to affect the business outcome?
- What responsibility must stay with the business manager?

## Teach-Back

Without looking at the skill, answer:

1. How do you turn an HR symptom into a business diagnosis question?
2. How do you avoid over-personalizing a system problem?
3. What evidence would you check before recommending an HRBP intervention?

## Transfer Test

Take one real HRBP issue from work. Before using the agent, write:

- business target or pressure;
- visible HR symptom;
- likely people cause;
- likely role or manager cause;
- likely system or incentive cause;
- one evidence source;
- one intervention you would try first;
- what the business manager must own.

Then use the agent to critique your diagnosis and intervention.
